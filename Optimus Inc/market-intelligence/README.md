# Market Intelligence — Optimus's Own Market

Daily competitive intel on the market Optimus competes in. Web agencies. AI agent vendors. Productized service shops. The audience Optimus sells to.

## Scope distinction (read this first)

This hub is OPTIMUS'S OWN market intelligence. It tracks Optimus's competitors, audience, and pricing benchmarks.

Per-client market research is a different thing entirely. Each client project has its own `market-intelligence.md` file in its own repo, generated by the `/market-research` skill at the vault root. That file describes *that client's* market — their competitors, their local audience, their industry pricing — not Optimus's.

If you are looking for what to research for a new client, do not work in this folder. Run `/market-research` against that client's `initial-business-data.md`.

## Cadence

| Frequency | What | Where it lands |
|---|---|---|
| Daily | Competitor moves, content drops, pricing changes, new entrants | `daily-pulse/YYYY-MM-DD.md` |
| Weekly (Sundays) | Synthesis of the week's daily pulses + signal vs noise call | `daily-pulse/synthesis-YYYY-MM-DD.md` |
| As-discovered | Deep competitor analysis | `competitors/<competitor-name>.md` |
| Quarterly | Audience profile refresh | `audience/` |
| As market shifts | Pricing benchmark refresh | `pricing-benchmarks/` |

## Subfolders

### `daily-pulse/`
Daily snapshots. One file per day named `YYYY-MM-DD.md`. Keep these short — the goal is signal capture, not analysis. Anything noteworthy gets promoted to a competitor file or a synthesis note.

Format suggestion (not required):
- What competitors shipped, posted, or announced
- Pricing changes spotted
- New market entrants
- Audience signals (what the target market is talking about, complaining about, asking for)

### `competitors/`
One file or folder per major competitor. Deeper analysis lives here — positioning, pricing, claimed differentiators, actual deliverables, public client list, pricing leaks, weaknesses. Two categories to track:

- **Web agencies** — anyone selling small-business websites in the Optimus price band
- **AI agent vendors** — anyone selling chat assistants, voice agents, or marketing automation as a productized offering

### `audience/`
Optimus's target audience profiles. Segments, pain points, buying signals, where they hang out, what content moves them. Different from any individual client's audience research.

Likely segments to profile:
- Small-business owners shopping their first real website
- Owners with a current cheap site looking to "level up"
- Owners who already tried a freelancer and got burned
- Owners curious about AI but don't know where to start

### `pricing-benchmarks/`
What competitors charge for comparable scope. How Optimus is positioned (premium vs market). Specific data points (screenshots, scraped pricing pages, prospect-shared quotes from competitors) live here.

The output of this folder is the answer to "are we priced correctly" at any given moment.

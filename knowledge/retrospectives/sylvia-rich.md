## Content Notes

### What an honorary consul actually does (niche knowledge)
The honorary consul role was established under the Vienna Convention of 1969. In practice, the core services are:
- **Notarization** of documents
- **Authentication** of documents that need to be valid in Hungary
- **Apostille** guidance (vs. authentication vs. notarization — all three are different)
- **Document translation** facilitation
- **Citizenship/passport documentation support** (not issuing; validating supporting docs)

The honorary consul does NOT:
- Issue passports (goes to consulate general in NY or DC)
- Handle citizenship applications directly (separate process through Hungary directly or NY)
- Charge fees (volunteer, non-paid, non-political)

### Most common client inquiry (biggest time drain)
> "Dear Sylvia, my name is Amanda Smith and I would like a Hungarian passport."

The quiz and blog exist to filter and answer this before Sylvia gets the email.

### The citizenship path (from Sylvia's own experience)
For first-generation Americans:
1. Register birth in Hungary — U.S. birth certificate translated to Hungarian + validated → sent to Hungary via NY/DC
2. Register name changes (marriage, divorce) so Hungarian records match current legal name
3. Only after citizenship is confirmed → apply for passport

Sylvia's contact Agnes in New York handles this process for clients. The blog should explain this path and refer people to Agnes when appropriate.
